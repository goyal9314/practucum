Introduction:



All mechanisms are used to convert one form of motion into another. A mechanism is basically obtained from a kinematic chain. And a chain is made up of several links and pairs. Any to elements can be combined such that there is relative motion between the two. The relative motion may be one of rotation, sliding or cylindrical or globular. A chain is a collection of links and pairs. When we have four links with pinned contact or revolute pair betweern them (Relative angular motion) then we have a four bar chain as shown below.
Note here we have 4 links. And any two of the links are connected by a pinned joint forming a revolute pair. This implies that andy two links will have relative angular motion with the joint as a pivot. So in all there are 4 links and 4 pairs (Revolute). This is a four bar kinematic chain. In order to obtain useful predictable motion for a given input, we need to convert this chain into a mechanism. For this we need to fix one link forming a four bar mechanism.

Grashoff's Law:

The four bar chain has 4 links. So each of these links may be fixed to obtain a different mechanism. Each of these mechanisms are referred to as an inversion of the four bar chain. As there are 4 links we can obtain 4 mechanisms. Referring to the four bar chain shown below: s is the shortest link, l is the longest link and p and q are the remaining two links. In order to have at least one link having full rotation the links should satisfy Grashoff’s law where s+l <= p+ q.

Crank-Rocker Mechanism-

when the shortest link is adjacent to the fixed link as in figures, we obtain a crank rocker mechanism. The shortest link serves as the crank and the link q which is opposite to it has oscilating motion about the pivot as shown


Double Crank Mechanism-

When the shortest link is the fixed link, we obtain a double crank mechaism ie. both links p and l function as cranks.


Double Rocker Mechanism-

Finally, when s is opposite the fixed link, we obtain a double rocker mechanism. Links p and l have oscillating motion as shown.


Transmission Angle (μ):

The angle between output link and coupler is known as transmission angle.


The optimum value of the transmission angle is 90 degree. Since the angle will be constantly changing during the motion cycle of the mechanism, there will be a position at which the transmission angle will deviate most from 90 degree. In practice it has been found out that if the maximum deviation of the transmission angle from 90 degree exceeds 40 or 50 degree (depending on the type of application), the mechanism will lock. In certain cases this maximum deviation must be kept within 20 degree and in certain other applications maximum deviations of up to 70 degree may be permissible. One must consider the practical application of a mechanism in order to give a limit to this deviation (whenever in doubt, try to keep this deviation to less that 40 or 50 degree).
